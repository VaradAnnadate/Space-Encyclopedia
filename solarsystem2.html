Mars: It is the fourth planet from the Sun, often called the "Red Planet" due to its rusty-red appearance from iron in its soil. It is a cold, desert world with a thin, carbon dioxide-rich atmosphere, polar ice caps, and no liquid surface water, although it retains water as ice and in the thin atmosphere.

Jupiter: It is the largest planet in our Solar System and the fifth planet from the Sun. It is a gas giant, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, and features prominent colorful bands of swirling clouds and a persistent, immense storm known as the Great Red Spot.

Saturn: It is the sixth planet from the sun, a gas giant famous for its spectacular ring system. It is the second-largest planet in our solar system, composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, and the least dense planet.
